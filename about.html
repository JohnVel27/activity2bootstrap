About

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food consisting of small, round, golden-brown balls filled with diced octopus, tempura scraps, green onions, and pickled ginger. Originating from Osaka, this delicious snack is made by cooking a wheat flour-based batter in a specially designed takoyaki pan, which gives it its signature spherical shape. The cooked takoyaki is typically topped with a savory-sweet takoyaki sauce, Japanese mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed powder, adding layers of umami flavor. Loved for its crispy exterior and soft, gooey interior, takoyaki is a favorite at festivals, food stalls, and izakayas across Japan.
